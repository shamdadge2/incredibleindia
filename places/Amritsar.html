AMRITSAR

“Home of the Golden Temple and the spirit of Punjab.”

About Amritsar

Amritsar is the spiritual and cultural center of the Sikh religion. It houses the spectacular Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib), surrounded by the sacred Amrit Sarovar tank.

Known for its rich history, tragic past (Jallianwala Bagh), and vibrant street food culture, Amritsar offers a deeply moving experience of devotion and patriotism.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to March: Pleasant winter, ideal for visits
  • April to June: Hot summer, but evenings are okay
  • July to September: Monsoon season

Top Places to Visit in Amritsar

Golden Temple

The holiest Gurudwara of Sikhism, plated with gold.

Jallianwala Bagh

Memorial of the 1919 massacre, a solemn historic site.

Wagah Border

India-Pakistan border famous for the Beating Retreat ceremony.

Partition Museum

The world's first museum dedicated to the Partition of 1947.

Durgiana Temple

A Hindu temple with architecture similar to the Golden Temple.

Gobindgarh Fort

A historic fort now housing museums and cultural shows.

Important Precautions

  • Cover your head with a scarf inside the Golden Temple complex
  • Remove shoes and wash feet before entering the temple
  • Tobacco and alcohol are strictly prohibited near the shrine
  • Be prepared for crowds at the Wagah Border
  • Keep phones on silent in prayer areas

Things You Must Carry

  • Headscarf/bandana (men and women)
  • Modest clothing
  • Camera (allowed in most areas)
  • Water bottle
  • Walking shoes (slip-ons are easier for temple visits)

Travel Tips

Partake in the 'Langar' (community kitchen) at the Golden Temple. Try the Amritsari Kulcha and Lassi. Visit the Golden Temple at night to see it beautifully lit up.

How to Reach Amritsar

  • By Air: Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport (~11km).
  • By Train: Amritsar Junction is a major railway hub.
  • By Road: Excellent connectivity via NH1 (Grand Trunk Road).
