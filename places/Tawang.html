TAWANG

“Land of the Dawn-Lit Mountains and spiritual serenity.”

About Tawang

Located in Arunachal Pradesh at an elevation of roughly 10,000 feet, Tawang is known for its beautiful monasteries, crystal-clear lakes, and majestic waterfalls. It holds immense significance in Buddhism and offers untouched natural beauty.

Bordering Tibet and Bhutan, Tawang is a place of rich history and culture, offering peace and tranquility to every visitor.

Best Time to Visit Tawang

  • March to June: Pleasant weather, blooming flowers
  • September to October: Clear views, post-monsoon freshness
  • Winter (Nov-Feb): Heavy snow, very cold but scenic

Top Places to Visit in Tawang

Tawang Monastery

India's largest monastery and the second largest in the world.

Sela Pass

A high-altitude pass at 13,700 ft with the frozen Sela Lake.

Madhuri Lake

Also known as Sangetsar Lake, famous for its scenic beauty.

Nuranang Falls

A spectacular 100-meter high waterfall set amidst deep forests.

Tawang War Memorial

Dedicated to the martyrs of the 1962 Sino-Indian war.

Bumla Pass

The border pass between India and China (permit required).

Important Precautions

  • Inner Line Permit (ILP) is mandatory for entry
  • Acclimatize to avoid high-altitude sickness
  • Carry sufficient cash as ATMs are limited
  • Roads can be rugged; travel in sturdy vehicles
  • Respect military regulations and restricted areas

Things You Must Carry

  • Heavy woolens and jackets (very cold nights)
  • Permits and ID documents (multiple copies)
  • Basic medical kit and altitude meds
  • Power bank and extra batteries
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen for high-altitude sun

Travel Tips

Start your journeys early in the day as it gets dark quickly. Try the local Monpa cuisine, especially the butter tea. Be patient with road conditions; the journey is long but rewarding.

How to Reach Tawang

  • By Air: Nearest airport is Tezpur (Salonibari), ~300km away.
  • By Train: Nearest station is Rangapara North / Tezpur.
  • By Road: Drive from Tezpur or Guwahati (Assam) via Bomdila.
