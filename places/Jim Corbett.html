JIM CORBETT

“Where the tiger roams free in the foothills of the Himalayas.”

About Jim Corbett

Jim Corbett National Park in Uttarakhand is the oldest national park in India. Established to protect the endangered Bengal tiger, it is a haven for wildlife enthusiasts. Spanning hills, riverine belts, and grasslands, it offers a wilderness experience like no other.

Named after the famous hunter-turned-conservationist Jim Corbett, it was the first to come under the Project Tiger initiative.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to June: Park open to tourists
  • Nov-Feb: Winter, great for birding and clear views
  • Mar-Jun: Summer, best chance to spot tigers near water

Top Zones & Places

Dhikala Zone

The most famous zone, offering overnight stays and grassland views.

Bijrani Zone

Known for distinct forest cover and high probability of tiger spotting.

Jhirna Zone

Open year-round, popular for sloth bears and elephants.

Corbett Falls

A scenic waterfall surrounded by dense forest (picnic spot).

Garjiya Devi Temple

A sacred temple located on a large rock in the Kosi River.

Sitabani Forest

Buffer zone famous for bird watching and isolation.

Important Precautions

  • Never get out of your vehicle inside the park
  • Do not play loud music or shout
  • Avoid wearing bright colors; stick to earth tones
  • Carry valid ID proof for entry
  • Do not feed or tease the animals

Things You Must Carry

  • Camera and binoculars
  • Warm clothes (mornings are chilly even in summer)
  • Sun hat and sunglasses
  • Water bottle and dry snacks
  • Mosquito repellent

Travel Tips

Book permits online well in advance (Dhikala sells out months early). Consider staying inside the forest rest house for a real thrill. Respect the silence of the jungle.

How to Reach Corbett

  • By Air: Pantnagar Airport (~80km away).
  • By Train: Ramnagar Railway Station is the nearest railhead.
  • By Road: Drive from Delhi (approx 5-6 hours).
