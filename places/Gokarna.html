GOKARNA

“Where soulful temples meet pristine beaches.”

About Gokarna

Gokarna is a small temple town in Karnataka that has evolved into a chilled-out beach destination. It offers a quieter, more rustic alternative to Goa with its palm-fringed beaches and spiritual atmosphere.

Home to the Mahabaleshwar Temple, it attracts both pilgrims and backpackers who trek across its famous OM-shaped beach and other secluded coves.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Pleasant weather, ideal for beach trekking
  • April to June: Summer, hot and humid
  • Monsoon (Jul-Sep): Heavy rains, lush green but sea is rough

Top Places to Visit in Gokarna

Om Beach

Naturally shaped like the spiritual symbol 'Om'.

Mahabaleshwar Temple

A sacred Shiva temple housing the Atmalinga.

Kudle Beach

Popular for its cafes, sunsets, and yoga retreats.

Paradise Beach

A secluded beach accessible only by trek or boat (Full Moon Beach).

Half Moon Beach

A tiny, beautiful cove perfect for swimming.

Yana Caves

Unique limestone rock formations located nearby in the forests.

Important Precautions

  • Respect local culture; dress modestly in town/temples
  • Be careful while trekking on rocky paths
  • Swimming in some areas can be dangerous due to currents
  • No drugs/alcohol allowed near religious sites
  • Carry a flashlight if trekking back after sunset

Things You Must Carry

  • Good gripping shoes for beach trekking
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Modest clothing for temple visits
  • Insect repellent
  • Water bottle to stay hydrated

Travel Tips

Do the famous "Gokarna Beach Trek" covering 5 beaches. Stay in a beach shack for the authentic experience. Try the local South Indian thali.

How to Reach Gokarna

  • By Air: Goa Dabolim Airport (~140km) is the nearest international airport.
  • By Train: Gokarna Road station (10km from town) or Ankola.
  • By Road: Buses available from Bangalore, Mangalore, and Goa.
