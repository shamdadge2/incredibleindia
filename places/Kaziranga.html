KAZIRANGA

“Kingdom of the One-Horned Rhinoceros.”

About Kaziranga

Kaziranga National Park in Assam is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, hosting two-thirds of the world's Great One-Horned Rhinoceroses. Spread across the floodplains of the Brahmaputra River, it is a biodiversity hotspot featuring tall elephant grass, marshland, and dense forests.

It is also a tiger reserve and home to elephants, wild water buffalo, and swamp deer.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to April: Park is open, weather is pleasant
  • May to October: Park usually closed due to monsoon floods
  • December/January: Great for bird watching

Top Zones & Places

Central Range (Kohora)

The most accessible zone, great for elephant and jeep safaris.

Western Range (Bagori)

Known for the highest density of rhinos, best for sightings.

Eastern Range (Agaratoli)

Famous for water birds and scenic wetlands.

Burapahar Range

offers trekking and sightings of Hoolock Gibbons.

Kaziranga Orchid Park

Biodiversity park showcasing local orchids and culture.

Assam Tea Gardens

Lush green plantations surrounding the park.

Important Precautions

  • Strictly follow the guide's instructions during safari
  • Do not get down from the jeep inside the park
  • Wear dull-colored clothes (green/brown) to blend in
  • Do not litter; plastic is banned inside
  • Maintain silence to avoid disturbing wildlife

Things You Must Carry

  • Binoculars and camera with zoom lens
  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Warm jacket for early morning elephant safari
  • Mosquito repellent
  • Dust mask (jeep rides can be dusty)

Travel Tips

Book safaris in advance, especially elephant rides (limited seats). Try the traditional Assamese thali. Early morning is the best time for rhino sightings.

How to Reach Kaziranga

  • By Air: Jorhat Airport (97km) or Guwahati Airport (217km).
  • By Train: Furkating Junction (75km) or Guwahati.
  • By Road: Located on NH37, well connected by bus/taxi.
