RISHIKESH

“The Gateway to the Garhwal Himalayas and the Yoga Capital of the World.”

About Rishikesh

Nestled in the Himalayan foothills beside the Ganges River, Rishikesh is renowned as a center for studying yoga and meditation. It is equally famous for adventure sports like white-water rafting and bungee jumping.

Temples and ashrams line the eastern bank around Swarg Ashram, a traffic-free, alcohol-free, and vegetarian enclave upstream from Rishikesh town.

Best Time to Visit

  • March to June: Ideal for rafting and camping
  • September to November: Pleasant weather, rafting reopens
  • Winter (Nov-Feb): Cold but peaceful for yoga

Top Places to Visit in Rishikesh

Laxman Jhula

Iconic suspension bridge over the Ganges.

Triveni Ghat

A sacred ghat famous for the evening Ganga Aarti.

The Beatles Ashram

Historic ashram where The Beatles stayed; covered in graffiti art.

Neelkanth Mahadev

A sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva situated on a hill.

Parmarth Niketan

One of the largest ashrams, hosting the famous Ganga Aarti.

Ram Jhula

Another suspension bridge connecting Shivananda and Swarg Ashram.

Important Precautions

  • River currents are strong; risky to swim without guidance
  • Alcohol and non-veg food are prohibited in the holy town
  • Beware of monkeys on the bridges
  • Dress modestly in ashrams and temples
  • Listen to instructors carefully during rafting

Things You Must Carry

  • Modest clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • Walking shoes/sandals
  • Quick-dry clothes if rafting
  • Yoga mat (optional, many places provide)
  • Sunscreen and insect repellent

Travel Tips

Book your rafting slot in advance. Attend the mesmerizing evening Aarti at Parmarth Niketan. Visit the 'Chotiwala' restaurant for a traditional meal.

How to Reach Rishikesh

  • By Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (~35km away).
  • By Train: Rishikesh Railway Station or Haridwar Junction (~25km).
  • By Road: Bus/Taxi from Delhi (approx 6 hours).
