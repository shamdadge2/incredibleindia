SHILLONG

“The abode of clouds, culture, and rock music.”

About Shillong

Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, is a beautiful hill station situated on a plateau. Known as the 'Scotland of the East', it is famous for its rolling hills, waterfalls, and pleasant climate.

Shillong is also the rock music capital of India and vibrant with the culture of the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo tribes.

Best Time to Visit

  • September to May: Pleasant weather, blooming cherry blossoms (Nov)
  • June to August: Heavy monsoon, but waterfalls are majestic
  • October: Autumn festival season

Top Places to Visit in Shillong

Umiam Lake

A mesmerizing man-made reservoir offering water sports.

Elephant Falls

A three-tiered waterfall located amidst fern-covered rocks.

Shillong Peak

The highest point offering panoramic views of the city (ID needed).

Don Bosco Museum

Asia's largest museum of indigenous cultures.

Ward's Lake

An artificial lake with a wooden bridge and garden.

Police Bazar

The main market hub for shopping and local food.

Important Precautions

  • Traffic jams are common in the city; plan accordingly
  • Sun sets very early in the Northeast (around 4:30-5:00 PM)
  • Carry an umbrella always; rain is unpredictable
  • Respect local tribal customs
  • Book taxis in advance for sightseeing

Things You Must Carry

  • Light woolens (evenings can be cool)
  • Raincoat/Umbrella
  • Walking shoes
  • Motion sickness tablets (winding roads)
  • Power bank

Travel Tips

Take a day trip to Cherrapunji or Mawlynnong (Asia's cleanest village). Try the local dishes: Jadoh (meat and rice) and Doh Khleh. Listen to live music at a local cafe.

How to Reach Shillong

  • By Air: Shillong Airport (Umroi) is small; Guwahati (100km) is better.
  • By Train: Guwahati (Assam) is the nearest major railway station.
  • By Road: Scenic drive from Guwahati (approx 3 hours).
