HAMPI

“A city of ruins, boulders, and ancient glory.”

About Hampi

Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site in Karnataka, was the capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Set within a surreal landscape of giant boulders and palm groves, its magnificent ruins tell the tale of a bygone golden era.

From majestic temples to royal courts, every stone in Hampi has a story to tell.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to February: Pleasant winter, best for walking tour
  • November: Hampi Utsav (cultural festival)
  • March to June: Extremely hot, difficult to explore

Top Places to Visit in Hampi

Virupaksha Temple

The oldest functioning temple in Hampi dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Vijaya Vittala Temple

Famous for the iconic Stone Chariot and musical pillars.

Elephant Stables

A grand structure used to house the royal elephants.

Lotus Mahal

An elegant pavilion blending Hindu and Islamic architectural styles.

Anjaneya Hill

Birthplace of Lord Hanuman, offering panoramic views (climb required).

Hemakuta Hill

Dotted with ruins, it's the best spot for sunset views.

Important Precautions

  • Stay hydrated; the heat can be intense even in winter
  • Wear comfortable shoes (lots of walking involves)
  • Beware of monkeys, especially near temples
  • Do not scribble on or damage the monuments
  • Cross the river by coracle only with life jackets

Things You Must Carry

  • Sun hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen
  • Cotton clothes
  • Good walking shoes
  • Guidebook or map
  • Power bank

Travel Tips

Rent a bicycle or scooter to explore the ruins at your own pace. Visit the 'Hippie Island' (Virupapur Gaddi) for a relaxed vibe. Don't miss the sunrise at Matanga Hill.

How to Reach Hampi

  • By Air: Jindal Vijaynagar Airport (Vidyanagar) ~40km.
  • By Train: Hospet Junction (~13km) is well connected.
  • By Road: Overnight buses from Bangalore/Goa/Hyderabad.
