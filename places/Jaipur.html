JAIPUR

“The Pink City, where royalty is etched in every stone.”

About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is known globally as the Pink City. Part of India's famous Golden Triangle, it is a city of grand forts, opulent palaces, and vibrant bazaars. The city's architecture is a splendid fusion of Rajput and Mughal styles.

Steeped in history and folklore, Jaipur greets every visitor with warm hospitality and a glimpse into India's royal past.

Best Time to Visit Jaipur

  • October to March: Winter, pleasant weather for sightseeing
  • April to June: Summer, very hot (avoid if possible)
  • July to September: Monsoon, humid but less crowded

Top Places to Visit in Jaipur

Amber Fort

Majestic hilltop fort known for its artistic Hindu style elements.

Hawa Mahal

The 'Palace of Winds' with its unique 953 windows.

City Palace

The royal residence comprising courtyards, gardens, and buildings.

Jantar Mantar

UNESCO site featuring the world's largest stone sundial.

Nahargarh Fort

Situated on Aravalli hills, offering best sunset views of the city.

Patrika Gate

A stunningly colorful gate perfect for photography enthusiasts.

Important Precautions

  • Be cautious of unauthorized guides and touts
  • Agree on auto/taxi fares beforehand or use apps
  • Drink plenty of water to avoid dehydration
  • Dress conservatively in temples
  • Secure your belongings in crowded bazaars

Things You Must Carry

  • Comfortable walking shoes (forts require walking)
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat
  • Cotton clothes for day, light jacket for winter nights
  • Camera with extra memory cards
  • Power bank

Travel Tips

Visit Hawa Mahal early morning for the best light. Try 'Lassi' at Lassiwala and 'Dal Baati Churma'. Shop for jewelry and textiles at Johari Bazaar.

How to Reach Jaipur

  • By Air: Jaipur International Airport (Sanganer).
  • By Train: Jaipur Junction is well connected to major cities.
  • By Road: Excellent highways (NH48) from Delhi (5-6 hours).
