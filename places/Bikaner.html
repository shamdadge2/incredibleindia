BIKANER

“The red city where culture meets the desert dunes.”

About Bikaner

Bikaner is a vibrant desert city in Rajasthan known for its 16th-century Junagarh Fort, ornate havelis, and the unique Karni Mata Temple. It retains a medieval grandeur that permeates the city's lifestyle.

Often called 'Camel Country', Bikaner is famous for its best riding camels and is home to one of the world's largest camel research farms.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Cool and pleasant, Camel Festival (Jan)
  • April to June: Extremely hot summer
  • Monsoon: Warm with occasional rain

Top Places to Visit in Bikaner

Junagarh Fort

A formidable fort known for its intricate carvings and museums.

Karni Mata Temple

Famous 'Rat Temple' where thousands of rats are revered.

National Camel Centre

Research farm offering camel rides and camel milk products.

Gajner Palace

A lakeside palace-hotel surrounded by a wildlife sanctuary.

Rampuria Havelis

Known as the pride of Bikaner for their exquisite design.

Lalgarh Palace

An architectural masterpiece blending Rajput, Mughal, and European styles.

Important Precautions

  • Respect the rats in Karni Mata Temple (do not harm them)
  • Stay hydrated in the dry desert climate
  • Cover your head and dress modestly in temples
  • Be careful with food hygiene in street stalls
  • Check opening hours for museums and palaces

Things You Must Carry

  • Sun protection gear (hat, glasses, sunscreen)
  • Light cotton clothes; light jacket for winter nights
  • Camera with zoom lens
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Comfortable shoes for walking in forts

Travel Tips

Try the world-famous Bikaneri Bhujia and Rasgullas. Attend the Bikaner Camel Festival if visiting in January. Take a tonga ride through the old city streets.

How to Reach Bikaner

  • By Air: Nal Airport (domestic) or Jodhpur Airport (~250km).
  • By Train: Bikaner Junction and Lalgarh Railway Station.
  • By Road: Connected by NH11, NH15, and NH89.
