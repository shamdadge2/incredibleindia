ANDAMAN & NICOBAR

“Blue seas, white sands, and endless serenity.”

About Andaman & Nicobar

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands are a group of 572 islands in the Bay of Bengal. Famous for their turquoise waters, pristine white-sand beaches, and mangrove forests, these islands are a haven for beach lovers and divers.

Beyond the beaches, the islands hold historical significance with the Cellular Jail, reminding visitors of India's freedom struggle.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to May: Best weather, clear water for diving
  • January: Great for witnessing the turtle nesting
  • Monsoon (Jun-Sep): High tides, ferries may cancel

Top Places to Visit in Andaman

Radhanagar Beach

Ranked among Asia's best beaches, located on Havelock Island.

Cellular Jail

National Memorial monument famous for its sound and light show.

Ross Island

Ruins of British architecture overtaken by nature.

Elephant Beach

Popular for water sports like snorkeling and sea walking.

Neil Island

Known for its coral reefs and the natural rock bridge.

Baratang Island

Famous for limestone caves and mud volcanoes.

Important Precautions

  • Do not enter restricted tribal areas
  • Ferry schedules depend on weather; keep buffer days
  • Swimming is safe only in designated areas (croc danger in some)
  • Do not collect or damage corals/shells (illegal)
  • Carry motion sickness pills for ferry rides

Things You Must Carry

  • Beachwear, swimwear, and flip-flops
  • Sunscreen (reef-safe preferred), hat, sunglasses
  • Mosquito repellent (tropical climate)
  • Waterproof bag for electronics
  • Cash (ATMs can be unreliable on smaller islands)

Travel Tips

Book ferries (Makruzz/Green Ocean) in advance. Try the fresh seafood. Start your day early (sunrise is very early). Scuba diving in Havelock is a must-do experience.

How to Reach Andaman

  • By Air: Veer Savarkar International Airport (Port Blair).
  • By Sea: Ships available from Chennai, Kolkata, Vizag (3-4 days).
  • Inter-Island: Gov/Private Ferries and Helicopters connect islands.
