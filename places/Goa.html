GOA

“Sun, sand, sea, and a spirit that never sleeps.”

About Goa

Goa is India's smallest state and a global tourist paradise, known for its pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. From the bustling parties of North Goa to the serene tranquility of South Goa, it offers a diverse experience.

Rich in history with ancient churches and forts, Goa is also famous for its spicy seafood and laid-back 'susegad' lifestyle.

Best Time to Visit Goa

  • November to February: Peak season, perfect weather, festivals
  • March to May: Summer, good for water sports, fewer crowds
  • June to September: Monsoon, lush greenery, off-season rates

Top Places to Visit in Goa

Baga Beach

Famous for nightlife, water sports, and beach shacks.

Fort Aguada

A 17th-century Portuguese fort with a lighthouse and sea views.

Basilica of Bom Jesus

UNESCO site holding the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier.

Dudhsagar Falls

A majestic four-tiered waterfall located on the Mandovi River.

Palolem Beach

A scenic, crescent-shaped beach in South Goa known for silence.

Anjuna Flea Market

Vibrant market for shopping, food, and local vibes.

Important Precautions

  • Swim only in designated zones; heed lifeguard warnings
  • Stay hydrated and protect yourself from the strong sun
  • Agree on taxi fares before starting the trip
  • Be cautious with personal belongings on crowded beaches
  • Respect local dress codes when visiting churches/temples

Things You Must Carry

  • Swimwear and beach towel
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and hat
  • Light cotton clothes
  • Flip-flops or sandals
  • Driving license (if renting scooters)

Travel Tips

Rent a scooter to explore at your own pace. Try the Goan fish curry and vindaloo. Explore South Goa for peace and North Goa for parties. Carry cash for small shacks and markets.

How to Reach Goa

  • By Air: Dabolim (GOI) or Mopa (GOX) international airports.
  • By Train: Madgaon and Thivim are main railway stations.
  • By Road: Well connected by bus from Mumbai, Pune, Bangalore.
