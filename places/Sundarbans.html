SUNDARBANS

“Where the land ends and the mystery begins.”

About Sundarbans

The Sundarbans is a vast mangrove area in the delta formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna Rivers in the Bay of Bengal. It is the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Famous for the elusive Royal Bengal Tiger, which has adapted to swim in the saline waters, it is a mysterious land of tidal waterways and mudflats.

Best Time to Visit

  • September to March: Cool and pleasant, best for boating
  • April/May: Hot, but good for animal sighting near water
  • Monsoon: Avoid due to rough rivers and safety risks

Top Places to Visit in Sundarbans

Sajnekhali Watch Tower

Popular spot for bird watching and visiting the museum/turtle pond.

Sudhanyakhali

A watchtower offering good chances of spotting tigers and deer.

Dobanki Camp

Famous for its Canopy Walk, 20 feet above the ground.

Netidhopani

Known for its ruins of a 400-year-old temple and a watchtower.

Bhagabatpur

Home to a hatchery for Estuarine Crocodiles.

Burirdabri

Offers a mud walk experience and view of Bangladesh border.

Important Precautions

  • Entry into the forest is strictly prohibited without permit/boat
  • Do not carry plastic (Strictly Plastic-Free Zone)
  • Listen to the boatman and guide at all times
  • Do not dip hands/feet in river water (crocodiles/sharks)
  • Carry cash as there are no ATMs inside the reserve

Things You Must Carry

  • Sunscreen, hat, and sunglasses
  • Binoculars for spotting wildlife
  • Insect repellent
  • Light cotton clothes, but full sleeves recommended
  • Torch and power bank (electricity may be limited)

Travel Tips

Stay in an eco-resort for an immersive experience. Try the local honey and fish curry. Be patient; tiger sightings are rare and depend on luck.

How to Reach Sundarbans

  • By Air: Kolkata Airport (~112km from canning).
  • By Train: Local train from Sealdah to Canning (nearest railhead).
  • By Boat: From Canning/Godkhali to the islands.
