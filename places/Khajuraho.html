KHAJURAHO

“Where stone comes to life in a celebration of love and life.”

About Khajuraho

Khajuraho in Madhya Pradesh is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its group of monuments built by the Chandela dynasty. The temples are world-renowned for their stunning Nagara-style architecture and intricate erotic sculptures.

However, the sculptures represent only a small fraction of the art; the rest depict everyday life, gods, and mythical beasts with incredible detail.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Pleasant weather, ideal for temple walks
  • February: Khajuraho Dance Festival (Cultural extravaganza)
  • Summer: Very hot, best avoided

Top Places to Visit in Khajuraho

Kandariya Mahadeva

The largest and most ornate temple in the Western Group.

Western Group

The main cluster of temples, very well preserved (Ticketed).

Eastern Group

Includes Jain temples like Parsvanatha Temple.

Southern Group

Features the Dulhadev and Chaturbhuj temples.

Raneh Falls

A magnificent canyon formed by the Ken River (~20km away).

Panna National Park

Tiger reserve located nearby, great for a day trip.

Important Precautions

  • Hire only licensed guides (check ID)
  • Be firm with hawkers and beggars
  • Respect the monuments; do not touch the ancient sculptures
  • Wear slip-on shoes as you have to remove them often
  • Carry water during the temple tour

Things You Must Carry

  • Sun hat and sunglasses
  • Cotton clothing
  • Camera (tripods may require permission)
  • Audio guide app (optional)
  • Socks (to walk on hot stone floors)

Travel Tips

Don't miss the Sound and Light Show in the evening at the Western Group. Visit the Archaeological Museum nearby. Arrive early morning for the best photography light.

How to Reach Khajuraho

  • By Air: Khajuraho Airport (HJR) connects to Delhi/Varanasi.
  • By Train: Khajuraho Railway Station (5km away).
  • By Road: Buses from Satna, Jhansi, and Gwalior.
