COORG

“The land of warriors, coffee, and hills.”

About Coorg

Coorg (Kodagu) is a hill station in Karnataka renowned for its coffee plantations, steep hills, countless streams, and rich flora/fauna. It is known as the 'Scotland of India' for its stunning landscapes and pleasant climate.

Home to the brave Kodava warriors, Coorg offers a unique culture, spicy cuisine, and warm hospitality.

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Ideal for sightseeing and trekking
  • April to May: Pleasant summer, coffee blossom season
  • June to September: Monsoon, lush green but leeches abundant

Top Places to Visit in Coorg

Abbey Falls

A stunning waterfall surrounded by coffee estates.

Raja's Seat

A scenic viewpoint where kings used to watch sunsets.

Dubare Elephant Camp

Interact with elephants, bathe them, and enjoy boat rides.

Talakaveri

The source of the river Kaveri, a sacred pilgrimage spot.

Golden Temple (Namdroling)

Beautiful Tibetan monastery in Bylakuppe settlement.

Mandalpatti

Off-road jeep drive to a hilltop with breathtaking views.

Important Precautions

  • Be cautious of slippery rocks near waterfalls
  • Carry salt or repellent for leeches if trekking
  • respect the silence in the monasteries
  • Driving in fog can be risky; use fog lights
  • Book homestays in advance during weekends

Things You Must Carry

  • Light woolens for evenings
  • Rain gear (umbrella/poncho)
  • Trekking shoes
  • Insect repellent
  • Cash (ATMs limited in remote areas)

Travel Tips

Stay at a coffee estate homestay for the real Coorg feel. Don't forget to buy fresh coffee powder and panoramic spices. Try the 'Pandimeeri' (Pork Curry) if you eat non-veg.

How to Reach Coorg

  • By Air: Kannur (90km) or Mangalore (140km) airports.
  • By Train: Mysore (~120km) is the nearest major station.
  • By Road: Beautiful drive from Bangalore (~260km).
