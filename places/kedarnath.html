KEDARNATH

Where faith meets the mighty Himalayas

About Kedarnath

Kedarnath is one of the most sacred pilgrimage sites in India, located in the Garhwal Himalayas of Uttarakhand. It is home to the ancient Kedarnath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva and part of the revered Char Dham Yatra.

Surrounded by snow-capped peaks, glaciers, and spiritual calm, Kedarnath is not just a destination—it is a journey of devotion, endurance, and inner peace.

Best Time to Visit Kedarnath

  • May to June: Best weather for pilgrimage
  • September to October: Clear skies, fewer crowds
  • November to April: Temple closed due to heavy snowfall

Places to Visit in Kedarnath

Kedarnath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, over 1,000 years old.

Bhairavnath Temple

Guardian deity of Kedarnath, offering panoramic views.

Gandhi Sarovar

A serene glacial lake near Kedarnath.

Vasuki Tal

High-altitude lake known for spiritual significance.

Important Precautions

  • Acclimatize properly to high altitude
  • Follow weather updates strictly
  • Avoid travel during heavy rain
  • Carry medical essentials
  • Follow temple and local guidelines

Things You Must Carry

  • Warm clothes and rainwear
  • Comfortable trekking shoes
  • Valid ID and registration slip
  • Power bank and flashlight
  • Basic medicines

Travel Tips

Kedarnath involves a challenging trek, so physical fitness is important. Respect the religious atmosphere and natural environment. Avoid littering and always follow safety instructions.
