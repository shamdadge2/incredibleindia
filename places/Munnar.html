MUNNAR

“Endless tea gardens, misty hills, and pure bliss.”

About Munnar

Munnar is a breathtaking hill station in Kerala famous for its rolling hills covered in emerald-green tea plantations. Situated at the confluence of three mountain streams, it was once the summer resort of the erstwhile British Government in South India.

With its misty landscapes, exotic flora like the Neelakurinji (blooming once in 12 years), and diverse wildlife, Munnar is a nature lover's dream.

Best Time to Visit

  • September to March: Pleasant weather, clear views
  • April to June: Mild summer, great escape from plains
  • Monsoon (Jul-Aug): Magnificent waterfalls and mist

Top Places to Visit in Munnar

Tata Tea Museum

Showcasing the history and processing of tea in the region.

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and rolling grasslands.

Mattupetty Dam

Famous for its storage masonry dam and boating facilities.

Top Station

Highest point in Munnar offering panoramic views of Tamil Nadu.

Echo Point

A scenic spot where you can hear your voice echo through the hills.

Kundala Lake

A picturesque lake famous for Shikara boat rides.

Important Precautions

  • Drive carefully as roads are narrow and winding
  • Carry motion sickness pills if prone to it
  • Be prepared for sudden drop in temperature
  • Leeches can be common on treks during monsoon
  • Do not litter in the eco-sensitive zones

Things You Must Carry

  • Warm clothes (sweater/jacket) year-round
  • Umbrella or raincoat
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Camera
  • Power bank

Travel Tips

Stay in a tea plantation bungalow for an authentic experience. Buy locally made chocolates, tea, and spices. Wake up early to catch the mist rolling over the tea gardens.

How to Reach Munnar

  • By Air: Cochin International Airport (~110km).
  • By Train: Aluva or Ernakulam Railway Station (~110-130km).
  • By Road: Scenic drive from Kochi or Madurai.
