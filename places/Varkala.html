VARKALA

“Where the red cliffs kiss the Arabian Sea.”

About Varkala

Varkala, a coastal town in Kerala, is unique for its dramatic 15-meter high red cliffs adjacent to the Arabian Sea. It is a perfect blend of spirituality and beach culture, hosting the ancient Janardanaswamy Temple and a vibrant backpacker scene.

Unlike the flat beaches of Kerala, Varkala offers stunning cliff-top views, laid-back cafes, and pristine waters believed to wash away sins (Papanasam Beach).

Best Time to Visit

  • October to March: Pleasant weather, ideal for beach activities
  • April to May: Hot and humid, good for budget travelers
  • Monsoon (Jun-Sep): Heavy rains, but lush green surroundings

Top Places to Visit in Varkala

Varkala Cliff

A geo-heritage site lined with cafes, shops, and offering sunset views.

Janardanaswamy Temple

A 2000-year-old temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Kappil Lake

A serene estuary where the backwaters meet the sea.

Papanasam Beach

Famous for its holy waters and golden sands.

Anjengo Fort

A historical fort built by the British East India Company.

Ponnumthuruthu Is.

A secluded island in the backwaters with a Shiva temple.

Important Precautions

  • Currents can be strong; swim only in safe zones
  • Be careful while walking on the cliff edges
  • Dress modestly when visiting the temple areas
  • Stay hydrated as humidity can be high
  • Carry cash for small vendors

Things You Must Carry

  • Cotton clothes and beach wear
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and hat
  • Flip-flops and comfortable walking shoes
  • Mosquito repellent
  • Camera for the cliff sunsets

Travel Tips

Witness the sunset from a cliff-top cafe. Get an Ayurvedic massage. Try the fresh catch of the day at the cliff restaurants. Respect the pilgrims at Papanasam Beach.

How to Reach Varkala

  • By Air: Trivandrum International Airport (~40km away).
  • By Train: Varkala Sivagiri Station is well connected.
  • By Road: Buses and taxis available from Trivandrum/Kochi.
