VARANASI

“Older than history, older than tradition, older even than legend.”

About Varanasi

Varanasi (Kashi) is one of the world's oldest living cities and the spiritual capital of India. Located on the banks of the Ganges in Uttar Pradesh, it is a city of temples, ghats, and profound spirituality.

It draws millions of pilgrims who come to wash away their sins in the sacred river and to cremate their loved ones on the ghats, believing it leads to moksha.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to February: Cool winter, best for walking
  • October/March: Tansitory pleasant weather
  • April to June: Very hot and dry

Top Places to Visit in Varanasi

Kashi Vishwanath

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas, the heart of Varanasi's devotion.

Dashashwamedh Ghat

Famous for the spectacular evening Ganga Aarti.

Sarnath

Where Buddha preached his first sermon (~10km away).

Assi Ghat

A place where the Ganges meets the Assi river; popular for morning Aarti.

Ramnagar Fort

A Mughal-style fort located on the eastern bank of the Ganges.

Manikarnika Ghat

The main burning ghat, a place of intense reality and ritual.

Important Precautions

  • Beware of potential scams by boatmen or guides
  • Navigate narrow lanes carefully (avoid getting lost)
  • Respect cremation rituals; photography is often sensitive/prohibited there
  • Keep valuables safe in crowds
  • Drink only bottled water

Things You Must Carry

  • Walking shoes (best way to explore lanes)
  • Modest clothing for temples and ghats
  • Hand sanitizer and wet wipes
  • Small change for donations/beggars (optional)
  • Camera (but ask before clicking people)

Travel Tips

Witness the sunrise boat ride on the Ganges. Try the famous Blue Lassi and Banarasi Chaat. Experience the Ganga Aarti at least once.

How to Reach Varanasi

  • By Air: Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport (~26km away).
  • By Train: Varanasi Junction (BSB) and Deen Dayal Upadhyaya (DDU).
  • By Road: Well connected to Allahabad, Lucknow, and Patna.
