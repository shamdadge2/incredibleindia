JAISALMER

“A golden mirage rising from the heart of the Thar Desert.”

About Jaisalmer

Known as the Golden City due to its yellow sandstone architecture, Jaisalmer is a mesmerizing fort city in the Thar Desert of Rajasthan. At its heart lies the Jaisalmer Fort, a living fort where people still reside.

With its havelis, sand dunes, and camel safaris, Jaisalmer offers a quintessential desert experience steeped in folklore and beauty.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to February: Perfect weather, desert festival time
  • March to October: Very hot, generally best avoided
  • Winter Nights: Can get quite cold in the desert

Top Places to Visit in Jaisalmer

Jaisalmer Fort

UNESCO World Heritage site, one of the few living forts in the world.

Patwon Ki Haveli

A cluster of five ornate havelis showcasing intricate architecture.

Sam Sand Dunes

Famous for camel safaris, jeep bashing, and desert camping.

Gadisar Lake

A historic rainwater lake surrounded by temples and ghats.

Kuldhara

An abandoned 'haunted' village with a mysterious history.

Bada Bagh

A complex of royal chhatris (cenotaphs) with golden sunset views.

Important Precautions

  • Hydrate frequently; the dry heat can be deceiving
  • Protect skin from sun and sand
  • Book authorized desert safaris only
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly
  • Be careful of touts selling overpriced artifacts

Things You Must Carry

  • Sunglasses, scarf/hat, and strong sunscreen
  • Lip balm and moisturizer
  • Warm clothes for night camping (temps drop largely)
  • Power bank
  • Cash (ATMs might be scarce in dunes)

Travel Tips

Don't miss a night camp in the Sam Sand Dunes with folk dance. Try the 'Ker Sangri' vegetable dish. Enjoy a boat ride in Gadisar Lake at sunset.

How to Reach Jaisalmer

  • By Air: Jaisalmer Airport (seasonal connectivity) or Jodhpur (285km).
  • By Train: Jaisalmer Railway Station connects to Delhi/Jaipur.
  • By Road: Good roads from Jodhpur and Jaipur.
