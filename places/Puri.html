PURI

“The Land of Lord Jagannath and Golden Sands.”

About Puri

Puri is a coastal city in Odisha and one of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites for Hindus. Famous for the 12th-century Jagannath Temple, it is also known for its long stretch of golden beach on the Bay of Bengal.

Puri is a place where spirituality meets the sea, offering a unique cultural experience with its vibrant festivals, especially the Rath Yatra.

Best Time to Visit Puri

  • October to February: Cool weather, pleasant for beach and temple
  • June/July: Rath Yatra festival (very crowded but grand)
  • March to May: Summer, hot and humid

Top Places to Visit in Puri

Jagannath Temple

One of the most sacred Hindu temples (Non-Hindus not allowed inside).

Golden Beach

A Blue Flag certified beach known for cleanliness and golden sands.

Konark Sun Temple

UNESCO World Heritage site, marvel of ancient architecture (~35km away).

Chilika Lake

Asia's largest brackish water lagoon, home to dolphins and migratory birds.

Raghurajpur

Heritage crafts village famous for Pattachitra paintings.

Marine Drive

Scenic coastal road connecting Puri and Konark.

Important Precautions

  • Sea waves can be high; listen to lifeguards (Nolia)
  • Dress conservatively around the temple area
  • Beware of monkeys and stray cattle in streets
  • Negotiate prices with pandas (priests) and rickshaws
  • Electronic gadgets are strictly prohibited inside the temple

Things You Must Carry

  • Cotton clothes for the humid weather
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Lock and key for luggage (if traveling by train)
  • Comfortable footwear (much walking required)
  • Mosquito repellent

Travel Tips

Taste the 'Mahaprasad' (holy food) at the temple. Witness the sunrise at the beach. Visit Konark early morning to avoid the heat.

How to Reach Puri

  • By Air: Biju Patnaik Airport, Bhubaneswar (~60km away).
  • By Train: Puri Railway Station is a major terminus.
  • By Road: Good bus connectivity from Bhubaneswar and Kolkata.
