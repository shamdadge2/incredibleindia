VALLEY OF FLOWERS

“A vibrant fairy tale crafted by nature itself.”

About Valley of Flowers

Located in Uttarakhand, the Valley of Flowers National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its meadows of endemic alpine flowers and diverse flora. It creates a mesmerizing canvas of colors against the backdrop of snow-capped Himalayas.

It is also home to rare and endangered animals like the Asiatic black bear, snow leopard, brown bear, and blue sheep.

Best Time to Visit

  • July to August: Peak blooming season, vibrant colors
  • September: Flowers start fading, but greenery remains
  • June: Early season, some snow may persist

Top Places to Visit

The Valley

Walk amidst infinite blooming flowers and pristine nature.

Hemkund Sahib

A high-altitude Sikh pilgrimage site near the valley.

Ghangaria

The base camp for treks to Valley of Flowers and Hemkund Sahib.

Pushpawati River

The river flowing through the valley, adding to the charm.

Bhyundar Khal

A pass offering panoramic views of the surrounding peaks.

Nanda Devi Park

The neighboring national park with rich biodiversity.

Important Precautions

  • Trekking is required; moderate physical fitness is needed
  • Camping inside the valley is not allowed
  • Be prepared for rain; path can be slippery
  • Start the trek early to return before sunset
  • Do not pluck flowers or disturb the ecosystem

Things You Must Carry

  • Good quality trekking shoes
  • Raincoat or poncho (essential)
  • Trekking pole for support
  • Dry snacks, energy bars, and water
  • Warm layer (temperatures drop quickly)

Travel Tips

Stay at Ghangaria and trek to the valley daily. Hire a porter or mule if you are not comfortable carrying your pack. Check weather updates frequently as landslides can occur.

How to Reach Valley of Flowers

  • By Air: Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (~295km).
  • By Train: Rishikesh is the nearest railway station (~276km).
  • By Trek: Road ends at Govindghat; trek 13km to Ghangaria.
